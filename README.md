# 🔒 Big Data and Financial Privacy Research

This repository contains a comprehensive research report on data privacy within the financial sector in the era of big data. This project was conducted collaboratively as part of the "Assurance, Security, and Privacy (CIS 2103)" course.

## 🚀 Research Overview

Financial institutions process vast amounts of sensitive personal and financial information, making them prime targets for cyberattacks. The primary objective of this research was to examine the potential risks, difficulties, and best practices for protecting sensitive financial data when utilizing big data analytics. 

We hypothesized that the increasing use of big data in the financial industry poses significant risks to data privacy, necessitating strict privacy regulations and robust security measures.

## 📊 Methodology & Data Collection

To validate our hypothesis and gauge public perception, we conducted a structured research methodology:
*   **Survey Design:** Developed a comprehensive survey comprising yes/no questions, scale-based responses, and open-ended questions.
*   **Data Collection:** Gathered responses from 106 participants to understand their awareness and concerns regarding financial data privacy.
*   **Data Analysis:** Analyzed both quantitative and qualitative data to identify trends in customer trust and privacy expectations.

## 🔑 Key Findings & Concepts

Our research highlighted several critical insights aligned with core information security principles:
*   **The CIA Triad:** The findings strongly supported the need for Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability in financial data handling.
*   **Customer Trust:** 58% of respondents expressed discomfort sharing personal information with financial institutions, highlighting a significant trust gap.
*   **Transparency & Compliance:** A strong demand for transparent data practices and adherence to global standards like the GDPR.
*   **Security Recommendations:** The study advocates for advanced encryption, regular security audits, employee training, and the implementation of technologies like *Threshold Cryptography* to mitigate insider threats.
